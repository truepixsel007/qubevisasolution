Business and Investment Visas

For individuals who want to invest or start a business in Australia.

State Nomination

Some visas require nomination by an Australian state or territory government. Each state or territory may have its own list of occupations in demand.

Occupational Ceilings

There are occupational ceilings in place to limit the number of invitations issued for certain occupations.

Business Visa Options

Australia offers various visa options for individuals looking to engage in business activities. This includes the Business Innovation and Investment (BIIP) program, which comprises several visa streams such as Business Innovation, Investor, and Significant Investor visas. These visas are designed for individuals who want to establish, develop, or manage a business in Australia.

Company Registration

If you plan to establish a business in Australia, you will need to register your company with the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC). This process involves choosing a business structure, such as a sole trader, partnership, or company, and obtaining an Australian Business Number (ABN).

Taxation

Understanding the Australian tax system is essential for businesses operating in the country. The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) provides information on tax obligations, including Goods and Services Tax (GST) and corporate income tax.

Legal and Regulatory Compliance

Businesses in Australia need to comply with various legal and regulatory requirements. This includes employment laws, workplace health and safety regulations, and industry-specific regulations. Seeking legal advice is recommended to ensure compliance.

Workforce and Employment

If you plan to hire employees in Australia, you'll need to be familiar with the country's employment laws and regulations. This includes matters such as employment contracts, workplace rights, and occupational health and safety standards.

Industry Regulations

Different industries may have specific regulations and licensing requirements. It's important to research and understand the regulations relevant to your particular business sector.

Intellectual Property Protection

Protecting intellectual property is crucial for businesses. Australia has intellectual property laws in place, and registering trademarks and patents can help safeguard your business interests.

Trade and Investment Promotions

The Australian government actively promotes trade and investment. Various agencies, such as Austrade (Australian Trade and Investment Commission), provide support and information for businesses looking to expand into or invest in Australia.

Eligibility

You must intend to visit Australia for tourism, family visits, or other short-term non-work purposes.

Duration

The duration of stay allowed varies, but it is generally granted for 3, 6, or 12 months.

Multiple or Single Entry

The visa can be granted for a single entry or multiple entries during the validity period.

Work Limitations

This visa does not allow you to work in Australia, but it permits activities such as tourism, visiting family and friends, or other short-term non-work activities.

Application Process

The application process typically involves submitting an online application, providing supporting documents, and paying the applicable visa fee.

Health and Character Requirements

You might be required to undergo health examinations, and you must meet character requirements.

No Further Stay Condition

Some visas may have a "No Further Stay" condition, which means you cannot apply for another visa while you are in Australia. Make sure to check the conditions on your visa.

Electronic Travel Authority

Some passport holders might be eligible for an Electronic Travel Authority (ETA) or eVisitor, which are electronically linked to your passport and do not require a visa label.
